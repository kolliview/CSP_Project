Hotel Management course

There are various diploma and certificate courses in Hotel Management that a student can pursue after class 10. These Hotel Management courses after 10th help students learn the basics of the Hotel Management industry - Housekeeping, Catering, Front Office, Accounting, Administration, etc. Some of these courses are job oriented that can help students prepare for a career in Hotel Management after 10th.


Hotel management entails duties at a hotel or resort. It includes marketing, hotel administration, accounting, and housekeeping courses. As the tourism industry expands, more hotels and restaurants are being built around the world, thereby increasing the demand for competent hotel managers.

    Diploma in Hospitality Management:

    This diploma in hotel management after 10th prepares graduates to meet the industry's ever-changing needs by focusing on the fundamental concepts of hospitality, travel management, and the rapidly changing trends in these fields.     Diploma in Front Office Operations:

    This is a one-year diploma in hotel management after 10th. The front office, which includes the marketing, sales, and service departments, has direct contact with clients. The front office (also known as the front desk) in the hotel industry greets guests, takes and organises reservations, and allocates check-in and out of rooms. They also organises porter service, issue keys, and other security arrangements, and settle accounts. While primarily aimed at the hospitality industry, the course equips students with the knowledge, skills, and aptitude to work as a receptionist, front office clerk, or administrative assistant. The curriculum covers subjects like the Basic Operation of the Front Office, Accommodation Operation, and Business Communication.

    Duration:The duration of course is one year.

    Eligibility:A student must have passed class 10th with 45 per cent aggregate marks or equivalent.

    Fees:Rs 45,000

    Diploma in Food and Beverage:

    The Food and Beverage (F&B) division is the beating heart of the hospitality industry. The course is designed to provide students with fundamental food and beverage knowledge as well as skill sets. The F&B module's goal is to give students an understanding of the operational and supervisory aspects of running a food and beverage operation. Students will gain an understanding of food and beverage and its service in a variety of restaurant and establishment styles. They will also gain knowledge to create a broad plan for specific food and beverage operations.

    Duration:The duration of the course can range from six months to one year.

    Eligibility:A student must have passed class 10th or equivalent.
